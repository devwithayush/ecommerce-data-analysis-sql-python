# 🛒 E-Commerce Data Analysis (Olist Dataset)

Analyzes 100K+ e-commerce transactions to identify critical business challenges such as <10% customer retention, 7–8% delivery delays, and skewed product demand, by leveraging Python, SQL, and MySQL, and delivers actionable insights to improve business performance.

## 🚀 Project Overview

This project analyzes customer behavior, revenue trends, delivery performance, and product demand using real-world transactional data.

✔ Built a full pipeline:  
**Data Cleaning → Database Storage → SQL Analysis → Visualization**

---

## 📊 Dataset

- Source: Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset (Olist)
- 📦 100K+ Orders  
- 👥 90K+ Customers  
- 🛍️ 110K+ Order Items
- many more ...

After preprocessing, a final dataset with **100K+ records** was created for analysis.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

### 💰 Revenue Behavior
- Majority of transactions are below **500**
- Business relies on **high-volume, low-value orders**
- Few high-value transactions contribute disproportionately

---

### 👤 Customer Behavior
- **90%+ customers** are one-time buyers  
- Repeat rate is **<10%**  
➡️ Indicates **poor customer retention**

---

### 📦 Order Trends
- Orders show steady growth over time  
- Peak observed during **late 2017 – early 2018**  
➡️ Strong platform adoption trend

---

### 🚚 Delivery Performance
- Average delivery time: **10–15 days**  
- Late deliveries: **~7–8%**  
➡️ Even small delays impact satisfaction

---

### 🛍️ Product Performance
- Few products dominate sales  
➡️ **Pareto-like distribution (80/20 rule)**

---

### 🌍 Geographic Insights
- São Paulo (SP) dominates customer base  
- Other states contribute significantly less  
➡️ Demand is regionally concentrated
